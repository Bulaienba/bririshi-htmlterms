definitions of html5 and css terms

how to organize a webpage

article

an article represents an independent section of a website that can potentially be placed on its own on another site without losing context.

section

a section helps to organize a webpage. within each section similar content can be placed. the difference between articles and sections is confusing (for me at least!). i think of sections as nesting within an article. put another way, i think of the article as a book and the sections as the chapters... hopefully that makes sense.

div

a div can be used to orgnize a webpage as well. now i'm even more confused on the difference between a section and a div. i'm going to think of a div as within a section. so it goes article, section, div.

p tag

the p tag represents a block of text or a paragraph. in fact, this block of text is written with a p tag (and so are the other blocks of text you see on the page).

css easily allows different types of visual enhancements such as color, images, fonts. it also allows easy organization of everything on the webpage. without css it would be like a messy house with white walls and without furniture, flooring, and other design fixtures.
